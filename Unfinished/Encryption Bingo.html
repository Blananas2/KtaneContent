On the Subject of Encryption Bingo

I reckon this module will go well with retirement.

This module contains a modified version of the classic game bingo. When the bomb is initiated, and after every solve, a ball will roll out to the left of your 5 by 5 bingo card. the bingo ball will have encoded information written on it, referring to the position of a square on the bingo card. Stamp said position, and the ball will roll away. the module is disarmed whenever there are 5 stamps in a row. Keep in mind that the appropriate square to stamp may not always contribute to the line that solves the module. It is both possible and likely that multiple balls will appear per solved modulde, one after the other. Stamping the wrong square incurs a strike and gives you a new ball. Solving another module before all active balls have had their squares stamped also causes a strike, but does not give you a new ball.

If the writing on the ball is encoded in Morse Code, Tap Code, Maritime Flags, Semaphore, or Pigpen, stamp the square containing the encoded letter on the table below: (In which K is omitted. Morse Code balls will flash on their own, Tap Code balls are light blue and must be held down to hear the message.)

E L W G N
R O Z T B
U D P V C
X Q Y F M
H A J I S

If the writing on the ball is encoded in Lombax, Braille, Wingdings, Fontana, or Zoni, stamp the square containg the encoded letter on this table instead: (In which J is omitted.)

O Q T K U
N E V W I
X L M A P
G H Y C Z
S B F R D

If the ball has a Chinese number written on it, stamp that square in Chinese reading order. (Start at top right, up to down, then likewise with each column going left.)

Listening

If the ball has a white and black checkerboard pattern, it is a listening ball. Tap the ball to play a sound, and stamp the square that's in the same position in the table below as that sound:

Campfire Jaws theme Camera Dripping Water Fire Truck
Footsteps Paper Crumpling Sugar Sifter Clapperboard Singing Bowl
Audio Tour MTA Door Warning Balloon Deflating Egg Cracking Timpani
See-Saw Kinetic Sculpture Orchestra Tuning Walking on Snow Rain
UVB-76 Cat Purring Scissors Dishwasher Clock Chiming

If a ball has an arrow on it, stamp the first unfilled square that is in that direction from the square stamped before the previous ball. Arrows can point both orthogonally and diagonally.

If a ball has a number on it, count that many squares in reading order from the square stamped before the previous ball, then stamp that square. they can be in arabic numerals, roman numerals, or tally marks.
