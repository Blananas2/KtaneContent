On the Subject of Encryption Bingo

I reckon this module will go well with retirement.

When the bomb is initiated, and after every solve, a ball will roll out to the right of a 5 by 5 bingo card. The bingo ball will have encoded information written on it, referring to the position of a square on the card. Stamp said position, and the ball will roll away. The module is disarmed once there are 5 stamps in a row, column, or diagonal line. It is possible that multiple balls will appear per solved module, one after the other. Stamping the wrong square incurs a strike and gives you a new ball. Solving another module before all active balls have had their squares stamped also causes a strike, but does not give you a new ball.

If the writing on the ball is encoded in Morse Code, Tap Code, Maritime Flags, Semaphore, or Pigpen, stamp the square containing the encoded letter on the table below: (In which K is omitted. Morse Code balls will flash on their own, Tap Code balls are light blue and must be held down to hear the message.)

E L W G N
R O Z T B
U D P V C
X Q Y F M
H A J I S

If the writing on the ball is encoded in Lombax, Braille, Wingdings, Zoni, or Fontana, stamp the square containg the encoded letter on this table instead: (In which J is omitted.)

O Q T K U
N E V W I
X L M A P
G H Y C Z
S B F R D

If the ball has a white and black checkerboard pattern, it is a listening ball. Tap the ball to play a sound, and stamp the square that's in the same position in the table below as that sound.

Campfire Jaws theme Camera Dripping Water Fire Truck

If a ball has an arrow on it, stamp the first unfilled square that is in that direction from the most recently stamped square, looping over if needed. Arrows can point both orthogonally and diagonally.

If a ball has a number on it, stamp the square that is that many squares in reading order from the most recently stamped square. Numbers can take the form of Arabic numerals, Roman numerals, binary, or Zoni.

If the ball has a Chinese number written on it, stamp that square in Chinese reading order. (Start at top right, up to down, then likewise with each column going left.)
